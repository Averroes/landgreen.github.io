Wave Properties

Waves have some properties that aren't always intuitive.

  • Waves only transmit energy, not matter.
  • Multiple waves can be in the same position at the same time.
  • Waves move at a constant speed determined by their medium.
  • Waves expand in all directions.

    • Interference

    When two waves are in the same position we say they are in superposition . Unlike particles, waves pass through each other without any effect.

    For waves in superposition the observed amplitude is the sum of each wave's amplitude.

    Waves can add constructively where the resulting amplitude is higher. An example of this would be two speakers playing the same song.

    Waves can also add destructively where the resulting amplitude is lower. An example of this would be noise cancelling headphones.

    Sound waves at different frequencies interfere to produce music.

    The Wedge is a famous surf spot that has some dangerously fun waves because of constructive interference from a wave reflected off a jetty.

    $$ 0.3m - 0.3m = 0m$$

    Diffraction

    Waves tend to spread out in every direction possible within their medium. This behavior is very noticeable when a wave hits a wall.

    Diffraction describes how a wave will spread out after passing through a small gap. This explains how sound can be heard through an open door even when there is no line of sight. Perhaps the clearest example of diffraction is water waves going around a gap.

    • Reflection and refraction occur when the wave speed changes as it enters a new medium.

    Reflection = change in direction of a wave after hitting a barrier.

    angle of incidence = angle of reflection

    When light strikes a bumpy surface it scatters in multiple directions, producing a diffuse reflection. Some materials produce a clear mirror image, called specular reflection.

    Refraction = bending of a wave after a change in the speed of the wave

    Snell's Law

    $$\frac{sin \theta_1}{sin \theta_2} = \frac{v_1}{v_2} = \frac{ \lambda_1}{\lambda_2} =\frac{n_2}{n_1} $$

    \( \theta_1 \) = angle of incidence
    \( \theta_2 \) = angle of refraction
    \(v\) = propagation speed [m/s]
    \(\lambda\) = wavelength [m, meters]
    \(n\) = index of refraction [no units]
