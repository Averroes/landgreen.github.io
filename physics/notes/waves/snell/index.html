Reflection and Refraction

Waves and Barriers

Reflection and refraction occur when the wave speed changes as it enters a new medium.

Reflection = change in direction of a wave after hitting a barrier.

angle of incidence = angle of reflection

When light strikes a bumpy surface it scatters in multiple directions, producing a diffuse reflection. Some materials produce a clear mirror image, called specular reflection.

Refraction = bending of a wave after a change in the speed of the wave

When a wave is reflected the interference pattern can produce Standing waves.

Refraction also makes rainbows! Wow!

Snell's Law

Several consistent ratios hold for light as it refracts.

$$\frac{sin \theta_1}{sin \theta_2} = \frac{v_1}{v_2} = \frac{ \lambda_1}{\lambda_2} =\frac{n_2}{n_1} $$

\( \theta_1 \) = angle of incidence
\( \theta_2 \) = angle of refraction
\(v\) = propagation speed [m/s]
\(\lambda\) = wavelength [m, meters]
\(n\) = index of refraction [no units]

The speed and wavelength change with the medium, but the frequencies doesn't because it is determined by the source of the wave.

As the light wave enters a new medium some of the light is reflected, and some is refracted. If the angle of refraction is 90º or higher, the light will only reflect. This is called total internal reflection. The angle of incidence that produces a 90º angle of refraction is called the critical angle.

You can see total internal reflection if you swim under some water and look upwards.

Example: A 600nm wave of red light starts in water and enters air. What is the critical angle for total internal reflection.
hint
Look up the index of refraction for air and water.
Set the angle of refraction to 90º and solve for the angle of incidence.

solution

1 = water       2 = air

$$\frac{sin \theta_1}{sin\theta_2} = \frac{n_2}{n_1} $$ $$sin \theta_1 = \frac{n_2 sin\theta_2}{n_1} $$ $$sin^{-1} (sin\theta_1) = sin^{-1} \left(\frac{n_2 sin\theta_2}{n_1}\right)$$ $$\theta_1 = sin^{-1} \left(\frac{n_2 sin\theta_2}{n_1}\right)$$ $$\theta_1 = sin^{-1} \left(\frac{(1) sin(90)}{1.333}\right)$$ $$\theta_1 = sin^{-1} \left(\frac{1}{1.333}\right)$$ $$\theta_1 = 48.6\degree$$
